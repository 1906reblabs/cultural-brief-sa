Privacy & Cookie Policy

Last updated: April 2026  ·  Governed by the laws of the Republic of South Africa

This policy is short, plain-language, and complete. It tells you exactly what data this website and newsletter collects, why, and what your rights are under the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (POPIA).

2. What personal information we collect and why

2.1 Newsletter subscribers

If you subscribe via Substack, Substack collects your email address on our behalf. We use it solely to deliver The Cultural Brief (SA) to your inbox every Thursday. We do not sell, rent, or share your email address with any third party. We do not use it for any purpose other than delivering the newsletter.

Substack may collect additional data (IP address at subscription, device type, open and click rates) for their platform analytics. Substack's own Privacy Policy governs their data practices. We have no access to individual open or click data per subscriber.

2.2 Website visitors

This website is hosted on GitHub Pages. GitHub may collect server logs including IP addresses as part of their infrastructure operations. Their Privacy Statement governs this. We do not operate any server-side analytics, tracking pixels, or visitor identification systems on this site.

We do not use Google Analytics, Meta Pixel, or any third-party analytics or advertising tracking on this website.

2.3 Cultural partnership enquiries

If you contact us about a cultural partnership, we collect the contact information you provide (name, email, organisation) solely for the purpose of responding to your enquiry and administering any resulting partnership agreement. This information is stored securely and not shared with third parties.

4. Legal basis for processing (POPIA)

Under POPIA, we process personal information on the following bases:

  • Consent — newsletter subscription (you actively subscribe via Substack).
  • Legitimate interest — responding to partnership enquiries and administering partnership agreements.
  • Legal obligation — retaining records required by South African tax and contract law.

We do not process any special personal information as defined in section 26 of POPIA (race, ethnicity, health, religion, trade union membership, political views, criminal record, biometric data, or sexual orientation).

5. Your rights under POPIA

As a data subject under POPIA, you have the right to:

  • Access — request a copy of personal information we hold about you.
  • Correction — request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Deletion — request deletion of your personal information, subject to legal retention requirements.
  • Objection — object to the processing of your personal information.
  • Withdrawal of consent — unsubscribe from the newsletter at any time using the unsubscribe link in any edition, or directly through Substack. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.
  • Complaint — lodge a complaint with the Information Regulator of South Africa if you believe your rights have been infringed.

6. Data retention

We retain subscriber email addresses for as long as you remain subscribed. Upon unsubscription, Substack's data retention policies apply to their records. Partnership enquiry and agreement records are retained for a minimum of five years in compliance with South African contract and tax law requirements. Web server logs held by GitHub are subject to GitHub's own retention schedule.

7. Data security

This is a static website with no database or server-side user data storage. Personal information provided in connection with partnerships is handled via encrypted email. Newsletter subscriber data is held and secured by Substack under their enterprise security practices. We do not store subscriber personal information on any system we directly control beyond what is visible in the Substack publisher dashboard.

8. Cross-border transfers

Substack is a US-based company. Your email address and newsletter subscription data are stored on servers outside South Africa. This transfer is necessary for the provision of the newsletter service. Substack's contractual frameworks and privacy practices are the applicable safeguards. GitHub Pages is also US-hosted. By using this site and subscribing to the newsletter, you acknowledge that your data may be processed outside South Africa in accordance with section 72 of POPIA.

9. Children

This publication is directed at adult professionals and is not intended for persons under 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, contact us and we will delete it promptly.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or applicable law. The date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision. Material changes will be noted in the relevant newsletter edition. Continued use of the site or newsletter after a change constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

11. Contact

For any privacy-related queries, to exercise your rights, or to make a complaint, contact us by replying to any edition of The Cultural Brief (SA) on Substack, or via the Substack publication page. We do not publish a direct email address on the website to reduce spam exposure — a reply to any edition reaches us directly.
