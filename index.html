The Festival and the Language: What the KKNK Owes the Afrikaans It Was Built On

The KKNK opens in Oudtshoorn on Saturday — thirty-one years old and still in the middle of an argument about who the language it celebrates actually belongs to. The festival has changed. The question has not been resolved. The distance between those two facts is where the only interesting cultural work in Afrikaans is currently being done.

About this publication

One edition, every Thursday — The Cultural Brief (SA) is a deliberately scarce South African cultural newsletter edited for seriousness, not scale. It covers the arts, literature, heritage, and cultural policy with the calm authority of a trusted colleague, never the urgency of a feed.

Who it is for

Urban working professionals, practising artists, curators, academics, and culturally serious South Africans — in-country and in the diaspora — who value signal over noise. One edition is the product. Scarcity is intentional.

One edition. Every Thursday. Thank you for reading.
